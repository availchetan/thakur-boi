This is how you can try to handle toxic behavior from a senior team member:

🔍 First Step: 1:1 Conversations – Try to Understand the Underlying Cause

Before jumping to conclusions and actions, the first move should be an empathetic 1:1 conversation.

  • Objective: Understand why the behavior is happening.
  • Look for potential causes:
    • Burnout: Are they overloaded? Showing signs of fatigue, irritability, or disengagement?
    • Role mismatch: Are they doing work that doesn’t play to their strengths?
    • Personal challenges: Are there life events affecting their mood or focus?
  • Approach: Be non-confrontational, use open-ended questions like:

    “I’ve noticed some tension in recent meetings. How are things going on your end?”

    “What’s been feeling most challenging in your work lately?”

⚠️ This phase is about listening, not judging. Show concern for their well-being while observing patterns.

  • Set Clear Expectations:
    • Define what “success” looks like in specific, measurable terms.
    • Examples: “All code reviews completed in 48 hours,” or “Professional tone maintained in standups.”
  • Timeline: Typically 4–6 weeks.
  • Structure:
    • Weekly check-ins to review progress.
    • Provide feedback and support (coaching, resources, etc.).
    • Encourage questions and two-way dialogue.

✨ Emphasize the goal is to support them in improving, not to set them up to fail.

🚩 If Behavior is Toxic (e.g., Undermining, Hostile, Disrespectful)

Some behaviors cross the line and affect team culture and morale. In such cases:

  • 1. Give Direct Feedback – Use the SBI Model:
    • Situation: “In yesterday’s team meeting…”
    • Behavior: “…you interrupted two junior members and dismissed their ideas abruptly…”
    • Impact: “…which made them hesitant to contribute and visibly discouraged.”
  • 🎯 Focus on observable behavior, not assumptions or character judgments.
  • 2. Loop in HR or Skip-Level Manager Early:
    • Escalate early if behavior continues despite feedback.
    • HR can help document, mediate, and initiate formal actions if necessary.

🚪 If No Improvement → Transition Out Quickly

If there's no meaningful improvement:

  • Begin formal offboarding or role transition (in consultation with HR).
  • Prioritize team morale and psychological safety.
  • Be transparent but discreet with the team—acknowledge the change without airing details.

📝 Always Document Everything

Keep a record of:

  • Dates of conversations
  • Observed behaviors
  • Feedback given
  • Responses/actions taken

This protects you and ensures fairness in decision-making.

💡 Tone Matters: Be Clear, Kind, and Firm

You can be empathetic without being permissive. Use a tone that:

  • Communicates care for the individual
  • Prioritizes team health
  • Is honest about what will and won’t be tolerated
